Efo-Riro

Efo Riro is one of the most popular vegetable soups in Nigeria and an absolute favourite of the Yorubas

Ingredients

  • Beef
  • Cow tripe (Shaki)
  • Cowskin (Ponmo)
  • Dry fish
  • Palm oil (20 cl)
  • Efo Shoko (500g)
  • 3 medium /2 big size bell peppers (Tatashe)
  • 11/2scotch bonnet (Rodo)
  • 3 tbsp ground crayfish
  • 1Â½ Onions
  • 2 Seasoning cubes
  • 2 tbsp of locust beans (iru)
  • Salt

Cooking Guide

How to prepare your ingredients

  1. Wash and cut the vegetable into big chunk. Blanch vegetable in hot water for about 3 minutes drain and set aside.
  2. Wash locust beans with just water and set aside
  3. Soak the dry fish in water till soft and debone
  4. De-seed pepper and roughly blend

Steps

  1. Start cooking the shaki first with as little water as possible as it is the toughest meat in the bunch.Add more water in small quantity as it cooks.
  2. When the shaki starts to curl, add the dry fish and stockfish.
  3. When the shaki is almost done, add beef, seasoning cubes and some of the onions and cook till all the meat and fish are well done. Set these aside.
  4. In another pot, pour the palm oil and heat it. Once hot, add the remaining onions and fry for 2 minutes.
  5. Then add locust beans fry for a minute. Now pour your blended pepper mixture, and stir together. Add salt, seasoning cube and crayfish. Allow to cook for about 20 minutes.
  6. Once the oil is floating at the top, add smocked fish, ponmo, and meats. Stir and taste for salt.
  7. Add the vegetables to the sauce and stir very well, cover the pot and allow to cook for about 4 minutes. After some minutes, take the pot off the heat.
  8. Your Efo riro is ready
